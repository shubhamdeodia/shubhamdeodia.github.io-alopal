Introduction

AloPal Copilot is a proof‑of‑concept healthcare AI copilot focused on quantitative hair‑loss tracking, prior authorization automation, and patient engagement in alopecia care. It was developed as an innovative solution during a 24–48 hour hackathon, targeting the fragmented and inefficient workflows in managing alopecia (hair loss) patients. AloPal can function as a standalone application or as an extension to existing dermatology clinical software. This report provides a detailed analysis of AloPal Copilot’s objectives, design, and potential impact, along with additional research‑backed insights and recommendations to enhance the original concept.

Problem context: challenges in alopecia care and coverage

  • Fragmented care across providers. Patients often see multiple specialists — dermatologists, PCPs, endocrinologists — which fragments coordination and leads to inconsistent treatment approaches.
  • Subjective tracking of disease progression. Photographs vary in lighting and angle; standardized metrics like SALT exist but aren’t routinely calculated in busy practice.
  • Cumbersome prior authorization for treatments. PAs delay access to care and create heavy administrative burden, particularly for expensive therapies like JAK inhibitors.
  • Unstructured data hides key evidence. Crucial facts in notes and PDFs are hard to retrieve, slowing auth packets and research.

Why it matters: delays in approvals, clinician burnout from paperwork, and loss of clinical insights. Streamlining measurable tracking and automating prior auth improves time‑to‑therapy and satisfaction.

Goals and objectives

Measurable tracking of hair loss, reduced manual effort, faster approvals, and a better patient experience.

Proposed solution (MVP overview)

Image analysis for hair metrics. Calculate density, diameter bands, and region maps from scalp photos. NLP of notes. Extract facts and create structured outputs from intake notes and transcripts. Draft clinical note. Generate a suggested SOAP with codes. Generate prior‑auth packet. Compile payer‑ready forms with evidence. Patient summary. Provide plain‑language updates and reminders.

End‑to‑end workflow

1) Patient check‑in and capture → 2) AI analysis and suggestions → 3) One‑click documentation and orders → 4) Longitudinal progress view → 5) Patient portal update.

System architecture (Azure‑powered)

Front‑end in React/Next.js; Azure AI Speech and Azure OpenAI for NLP; custom CV via Azure ML; FHIR in Azure Health Data Services for interoperability; Event Grid + Functions + Logic Apps for orchestration; Fabric Lakehouse for analytics; Entra ID and standard Azure security controls.

Data model and FHIR mapping

Use Patient, Condition, Observation, Procedure, MedicationRequest, and DocumentReference to represent diagnoses, metrics (e.g., SALT), procedures, medications, and documents like prior‑auth packets and pathology reports.

Prior authorization automation features

Therapy‑specific checklists, auto‑populated forms, proof bundles with photos and graphs, and one‑click export/submission (PDF, FHIR bundle, or payer API). Human‑in‑the‑loop review stays mandatory.

Responsible AI and privacy

Clinician approval gates, single‑tenant deployments, no training on PHI by default, explainability with visual/text highlights, safety rails for out‑of‑scope findings, and full compliance controls.

Demo plan (5–7 minutes)

Upload data → show AI outputs (severity, region map, density table, SOAP, auth preview) → show patient view with side‑by‑sides and trend graph.

Success metrics and KPIs

  • Hair loss measurement accuracy: ≥ 70% vs annotated ground truth.
  • Processing speed: < 90 sec from input to draft note + auth packet.
  • Reduced manual steps: at least two tasks removed in demo flow.

Stretch goals (future enhancements)

  • Teledermatology triage with guided capture.
  • Home monitoring kit for standardized imaging.
  • Federated, de‑identified cohort queries.
  • Adopt emerging ePA standards (FHIR PAS).

Development plan and team

Dev 1: Vision pipeline; Dev 2: NLP pipeline; Dev 3: FHIR/data layer; Dev 4: Prior‑auth rules and submission; Dev 5: UI/UX.

Demo assets prepared

De‑identified image set, synthetic notes and reports, and payer rule templates with a mocked submission endpoint.

Key benefits

Objective decision‑making, reduced prior‑auth friction, and research‑ready, de‑identified data by design.
